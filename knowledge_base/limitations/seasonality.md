# Seasonality Hypothesis

Vegetation varies naturally throughout the year.

Changes in NDVI may reflect seasonal patterns rather than long-term environmental change.

## Why Seasonality Matters

Agricultural landscapes frequently exhibit:

* Planting periods
* Growth periods
* Harvest periods

These cycles significantly affect vegetation indices.

## Common Seasonal Effects

Examples:

* Lower NDVI after harvest
* Higher NDVI during active growing seasons
* Temporary reductions during dry periods

## Supporting Evidence

Seasonality becomes more plausible when:

* Only a single observation is available
* Agricultural activity dominates the region
* Historical vegetation patterns fluctuate seasonally

## Contradicting Evidence

Seasonality becomes less likely when:

* Vegetation decline persists across multiple years
* Land use changes significantly
* Multiple observations show consistent degradation

## Interpretation Guidance

Single-date NDVI observations should be interpreted cautiously.

Long-term temporal analysis provides much stronger evidence than a single snapshot.
