# Limitations of Water Proximity Analysis

Distance to water bodies is a useful contextual signal but has important limitations.

## Water Proximity Is Not Water Availability

A nearby lake does not guarantee:

* Water accessibility
* Water quality
* Groundwater recharge
* Irrigation support

## Surface Water vs Groundwater

Vegetation health may depend more heavily on groundwater than visible surface water.

Surface water proximity therefore represents only part of the environmental picture.

## Seasonal Variability

Many lakes and ponds exhibit seasonal fluctuations.

A mapped water body may contain limited water during dry periods.

## Human Influences

Water bodies may be:

* Polluted
* Encroached
* Poorly maintained
* Hydrologically disconnected

## Interpretation Guidance

Water proximity should be interpreted as contextual evidence rather than direct proof of environmental resilience.

## Example

Observation:

Low NDVI

Distance to nearest lake = 300 meters

Interpretation:

Nearby water weakens the water-scarcity hypothesis but does not eliminate it entirely. Additional evidence is required to understand the true cause of vegetation conditions.

## Recommended Usage

Water proximity should be combined with:

* NDVI
* Land use
* Temporal trends
* Rainfall information

to support stronger environmental reasoning.
