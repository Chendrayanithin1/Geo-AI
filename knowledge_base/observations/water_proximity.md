# Water Bodies and Environmental Resilience

Bengaluru historically developed around an interconnected system of lakes and tanks designed for water storage and groundwater recharge.

Surface water bodies influence local environmental conditions through:

* Groundwater recharge
* Soil moisture support
* Local temperature moderation
* Ecosystem support

## Why Water Proximity Matters

Regions located closer to lakes and ponds may have improved access to surface water resources.

However, proximity alone does not guarantee environmental resilience.

## Important Limitations

A nearby water body may:

* Be seasonal
* Be polluted
* Have low storage capacity
* Be disconnected from agricultural activity

Therefore, distance to water should be interpreted as contextual evidence rather than a direct measure of water availability.

## Interpreting Combined Signals

Low NDVI + Large Distance to Water

Possible interpretation:
Vegetation decline may be influenced by limited access to surface water.

Low NDVI + Small Distance to Water

Possible interpretation:
Water scarcity alone may not explain vegetation conditions. Alternative explanations such as urbanization, land-use change, or seasonal effects should be investigated.

The relationship between vegetation health and water proximity should always be treated as probabilistic rather than deterministic.
