# Urban Expansion and Vegetation Change in Bengaluru

Bengaluru has undergone significant urban expansion over the last two decades. Growth corridors have extended into previously agricultural and semi-rural regions.

Areas such as Anekal, Devanahalli, Hoskote, Whitefield, Sarjapur, and Electronic City have experienced rapid residential, industrial, and infrastructure development.

## Impact on Vegetation

Urbanization frequently causes a reduction in vegetation cover due to:

* Construction of residential layouts
* Expansion of road networks
* Industrial development
* Commercial infrastructure
* Land conversion

This often results in lower NDVI values even when water resources are available.

## Interpreting Low NDVI in Bengaluru

When low NDVI is observed in rapidly developing regions, urbanization should be considered as a potential explanation.

Low NDVI in a peri-urban region may reflect:

* Land-use conversion
* Construction activity
* Infrastructure development

rather than drought or ecological degradation.

## Evidence Supporting Urbanization Hypothesis

* Low NDVI
* Presence of urban growth corridors
* Proximity to industrial zones
* Ongoing development activity

## Evidence Against Urbanization Hypothesis

* Stable land use over time
* Significant NDVI decline without corresponding development activity

Urbanization is therefore a plausible explanation but should not be treated as definitive without additional evidence.
