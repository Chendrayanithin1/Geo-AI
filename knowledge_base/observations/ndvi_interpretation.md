# Understanding NDVI in Environmental Intelligence

Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) is one of the most widely used indicators of vegetation health derived from satellite imagery. It uses the difference between Near Infrared (NIR) and Red light reflectance to estimate vegetation density and photosynthetic activity.

## Typical NDVI Ranges

### NDVI > 0.7

Represents dense and healthy vegetation. These values are commonly observed in forests, parks, and areas with abundant vegetation cover.

### NDVI 0.4 - 0.7

Represents moderate vegetation cover. Agricultural land, grasslands, and mixed urban-green regions often fall within this range.

### NDVI 0.2 - 0.4

Represents sparse vegetation. Areas may contain limited vegetation cover, dry grasslands, recently harvested agricultural fields, or partially urbanized landscapes.

### NDVI < 0.2

Represents very sparse vegetation, built-up regions, barren land, exposed soil, or potentially stressed vegetation.

## What Low NDVI Can Mean

Low NDVI does not automatically indicate environmental degradation. Possible explanations include:

* Urban development
* Construction activity
* Industrial zones
* Seasonal agricultural cycles
* Natural barren land
* Vegetation stress
* Water stress

## Important Limitation

NDVI measures vegetation condition but does not explain why vegetation is low. Additional evidence is required to determine causality.

A low NDVI observation should be treated as a signal requiring investigation rather than a final conclusion.
