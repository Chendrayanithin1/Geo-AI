# Vegetation Stress Hypothesis

Vegetation stress refers to a reduction in vegetation health caused by environmental, climatic, or ecological pressures. It is one of the most common explanations considered when low NDVI values are observed.

## Indicators

Potential indicators include:

* Low NDVI values
* Declining NDVI trends over time
* Reduced greenness compared to neighboring regions
* Sparse vegetation cover

## Potential Causes

Vegetation stress may arise from:

* Water scarcity
* Reduced soil moisture
* Heat stress
* Land degradation
* Ecosystem disturbances
* Human activity

## Supporting Evidence

The vegetation stress hypothesis becomes more plausible when:

* NDVI is significantly lower than surrounding regions
* NDVI has declined over multiple observations
* Water sources are limited
* Historical vegetation cover was higher

## Contradicting Evidence

Evidence that weakens the vegetation stress hypothesis includes:

* Nearby and healthy water systems
* Active agricultural cycles
* Known urban expansion
* Recent land development

## Interpretation Guidance

Low NDVI alone should not be treated as proof of vegetation stress.

Vegetation stress should be considered one possible explanation among several competing hypotheses.

Confidence should increase only when multiple independent indicators support the hypothesis.

## Example

Observation:

NDVI = 0.22

Possible Interpretation:

Vegetation health appears relatively low. Vegetation stress is a plausible explanation but additional evidence is required to determine whether the cause is ecological stress, urbanization, or seasonal variation.
