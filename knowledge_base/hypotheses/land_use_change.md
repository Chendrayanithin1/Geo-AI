# Land Use Change Hypothesis

Land use change occurs when land transitions from one functional purpose to another.

Examples include:

* Agriculture to residential development
* Agriculture to industrial activity
* Forest to infrastructure
* Open land to commercial development

## Relationship with NDVI

Many forms of land use change reduce vegetation cover.

This frequently results in lower NDVI values.

However, such changes do not necessarily indicate ecological decline.

## Supporting Evidence

Indicators supporting land use change include:

* Reduced vegetation cover
* Expansion of built-up areas
* Infrastructure development
* Industrial growth

## Contradicting Evidence

The hypothesis becomes weaker when:

* Historical land use remains stable
* No significant development activity exists
* Protected land dominates the region

## Interpretation Guidance

Land use change is often difficult to distinguish from vegetation stress using NDVI alone.

Additional context is typically required.

## Example

Observation:

NDVI = 0.25

Possible Explanations:

* Vegetation stress
* Urbanization
* Land use change

The system should avoid selecting a single explanation without supporting evidence.
