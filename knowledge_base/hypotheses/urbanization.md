# Urbanization Hypothesis

Urbanization is one of the most important alternative explanations for declining vegetation signals in rapidly developing regions.

In growing metropolitan areas, vegetation is frequently replaced by roads, buildings, industrial zones, and residential developments.

## Why Urbanization Matters

Satellite-derived vegetation metrics often detect the outcome of land transformation rather than the cause.

A low NDVI value in an urbanizing region may reflect:

* Construction activity
* Residential expansion
* Industrial development
* Transportation infrastructure

rather than environmental degradation.

## Bengaluru Context

Regions surrounding Bengaluru have experienced significant growth over the last two decades.

Examples include:

* Anekal
* Devanahalli
* Hoskote
* Sarjapur
* Electronic City corridor

These areas frequently undergo land-use transformation.

## Supporting Evidence

Urbanization becomes more plausible when:

* Low NDVI is observed near expanding urban regions
* Industrial activity is increasing
* Construction activity is visible
* Water sources remain accessible

## Contradicting Evidence

Urbanization becomes less likely when:

* Land use remains unchanged
* Protected green spaces dominate
* Historical imagery shows stable development patterns

## Interpretation Guidance

Low NDVI in a rapidly growing region should not automatically be interpreted as environmental stress.

Urbanization should always be considered as a competing explanation.
